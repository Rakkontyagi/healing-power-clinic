Neuro Rehabilitation

Stroke Rehabilitation

Recovery is possible at every stage. We create a dignified, safe program that supports mobility, independence, and caregiver confidencestep by step.

Program pillars

  • Early education: Positioning, transfers, and skin care to prevent complications.
  • Motor recovery: Facilitation techniques, task-specific practice, and graded strength and balance work.
  • Spasticity management: Comfortable positioning, prolonged gentle stretches, and home routines.
  • Caregiver training: Safe transfers, gait assistance, and daily activity strategies.

Our clinic is calm, accessible, and patient-centered. Sessions are paced to protect energy while nudging recovery forward.

FAQs

When should rehab start?

As early as safely possible, often soon after hospital discharge. Early, gentle practice prevents secondary issues and accelerates return of function.

How do you measure progress?

Functional milestones: bed mobility, transfers, standing tolerance, step counts, and self-care tasks. We track and celebrate each win.

Do you offer home plans?

Yes. We provide clear, written routines and train caregivers so daily practice is safe and effective.

Principles of recovery

Neuroplasticity thrives on specific, meaningful practice. We use task-oriented training, safe repetition, and clear goals to rebuild independence. Sessions respect energy and avoid overwhelming the nervous system.

Caregiver training

We teach safe transfers, gait assistance, and daily activity strategies. Written home plans make practice consistent between visits.

Spasticity management

Comfortable positioning, prolonged gentle stretches, and calming inputs reduce tone so practice quality improves.

Home safety

Simple modifications reduce falls: clear pathways, supportive footwear, and bathroom aids when needed.
